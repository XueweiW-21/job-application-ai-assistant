# Bio: Michael Gary Scott

## Career Story
I started in mathematics and statistics at the University of Scranton, then moved into applied ML during grad school at Penn State. My first industry role was at Dunder Mifflin's corporate analytics team, where I learned how to build data products for people who have never heard of a random forest and do not want to. Predicting which paper clients were about to churn taught me that the hardest part of data science is not the model, it is getting a regional sales manager to trust the model. At Lackawanna Health Partners, I found my sweet spot: applying ML to problems where the outcome directly affects people's lives. Healthcare gave me an appreciation for interpretability and stakeholder communication that I did not get in a corporate sales environment.

## What I'm Looking For
Roles where I can build ML systems that ship to production and have measurable impact. I care less about the specific domain and more about whether the team values rigorous methodology and clear communication. I want to work somewhere that treats data science as a decision-making function, not just a reporting one.

## Elevator Pitch
Senior data scientist with 5+ years building production ML systems in healthcare and analytics. I specialize in turning messy real-world data into models that clinicians and business teams actually trust and use. Published researcher, experiment design nerd, and the person who translates between the data team and everyone else.

## Writing Style Notes
- Direct and specific. Leads with the result, then explains the method.
- Avoids buzzwords. Says "built a model that reduced readmissions by 12%" not "leveraged cutting-edge AI to drive transformative outcomes."
- Uses concrete numbers whenever possible.
- Comfortable with technical terms but explains them when the audience might not know them.
- Tends toward shorter sentences. Rarely uses semicolons.
- Credits collaborators naturally: "worked with the clinical team" not "single-handedly revolutionized."
